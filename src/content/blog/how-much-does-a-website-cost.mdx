If you're thinking about building a website for your business, the first question you're probably asking is: **how much is this going to cost me?**

The honest answer: it depends. But in this article I'll break down real market prices so you can make an informed decision.

## What factors affect the price?

Before we talk numbers, let's understand what makes a website more or less expensive:

- **Type of website**: a corporate site is not the same as an online store
- **Number of pages**: more pages = more work
- **Features**: online bookings, multilingual, member areas...
- **Who builds it**: agency, freelancer, or DIY template

## Price ranges in 2026

### Small business or freelancer website

For a 5-8 page website (home, services, about, contact) built by a qualified freelancer:

**€800 – €2,500**

Includes custom design, basic SEO optimization and responsive design.

### Online store (eCommerce)

Depending on the number of products and features:

**€1,500 – €8,000**

The most important variables are product count, payment methods and ERP integrations.

### Agency or large enterprise website

Complex projects with multiple functionalities:

**€5,000 – €30,000**

## Freelancer vs agency: which is better?

Working with a freelancer has clear advantages for medium-sized projects:

- **Lower price**: no agency overhead costs
- **Direct contact**: you talk to the person doing the work
- **Flexibility**: easier to adapt to changes during the project

An agency makes sense when you need a multidisciplinary team for large projects or want corporate guarantees.

## What about maintenance?

Many clients forget this. A website needs:

- Security updates
- Domain and hosting renewal
- Content changes

Budget between **€50 and €200 per month** for basic maintenance.

## Conclusion

For a professional website that works well, ranks well and has careful design, the realistic range is **€800 – €3,000** for a freelancer or SME project.
